World's Air Quality

FACTS ON AIR POLLUTION


• Air pollution is the largest environment threat to human health worldwide.

• More than 99% of population live in areas where the air pollution is above WHO air quality guidelines.

• 8.43 million premature deaths annually attributed to air pollution.

• Air pollution causes $8.1 trillion in annual global health costs.

• 85% of all globals air poluution comes from burning fossil fuels and biomass.


Real-time Air Quality Monitoring Worldwide


  • Good

    0-50

    Air quality is considered satisfactory, and air pollution posses little or no risk

  • Moderate

    51-100

    Air quality is acceptable. However, for some pollutants there may be a moderate health concern for a very small number of people who are unsually sensitive to air pollution.

  • Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups

    101-150

    Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects.The general public is not likely to be affected.

  • Unhealthy

    151-200

    Everyone may begin to experience health effects. Members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.

  • Very Unhealthy

    201-300

    Health warnings of emergency conditions. The entire population is more likely to be affected.

  • Hazardous

    300+

    Health alert: everyone may experience more serious health effects.

How to protect yourself from poor air quality?


*AQI values at or below 100 are generally thought of as satisfactory. Starting from orange scale, the air quality is considered to be harmful*


1. Reduce the time you spend outdoors & reduce the intensity of outdoor activity.

2. If you must go outdoors, consider wearing a mask. You are advised to wear N95 or
KN95 masks as they can effectively filter out fine particles compared to cloth or dust masks.

3. Keep your air indoors healthy by keeping the windows and doors closed. Run air conditioning on the recirculate setting.
